1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document presents a comprehensive, law‑backed investment framework designed to systematically generate wealth on the Dar es Salaam Stock Exchange (DSE). The core philosophy rejects speculation; instead, it captures three permanent structural advantages unique to the Tanzanian market:

  • Zero capital gains tax on listed securities.
  • 5% withholding tax on dividends (versus 10% for unlisted companies).
  • Tax‑free interest on government bonds of ≥3 years and listed corporate bonds.

The DSE is a frontier market where 98% of participants are retail price‑takers. By acting as a disciplined liquidity provider through our proprietary "Shadow Market Making" strategy, and by layering in dividend‑capture, bond‑rotation, and legal‑arbitrage techniques, a starting capital of TZS 5,000,000 with monthly top‑ups of TZS 500,000 can realistically compound to over TZS 500 million within 6–8 years. This Board Pack is divided into two complementary parts: a practical strategy guide (Sections 2–3, 5–7) and a detailed legal reference (Section 4). Together, they form the complete operating manual for the Amana Capital East Africa investment engine.

Core Thesis: Structural tax advantages + disciplined liquidity provision + systematic reinvestment = a compounding engine that retail participants cannot replicate.

Position size is capped at ≤ 5% of a counter's 20‑day average daily volume. Primary counters: CRDB, VODA, DCB, NMB.

  • Place GTC limit buy order below best bid; GTC limit sell order above best ask.
  • Required gross spread: ≥ 2.4% to ensure net profit after all fees.

2. UNDERSTANDING THE DAILY MARKET REPORT

Every evening the DSE publishes a daily market report at dse.co.tz. Spend five minutes reading it. Here is what each section tells you.

2.1 The Headline Numbers

Total Turnover (TZS): How much money changed hands. Above TZS 3 billion = market is active, trade comfortably. Below TZS 500 million = quiet day — don't force trades.

Deals: Number of individual transactions. High count (2,000+) = many small traders active. Low count = institutional hands are moving.

Volume (shares): Combined with the deal count, this tells you whether flow is retail or institutional in origin.

2.2 The Counter‑by‑Counter Table

For each stock, the report shows Opening / Closing / High / Low prices, the VWAP (the most important number — average price weighted by volume, representing the session's "fair value"), total turnover and volume, and outstanding bids/offers at the close. We only trade counters with volume >5,000 shares/day. We anchor all buy and sell decisions to the VWAP.

2.3 Foreign Investor Flow

High foreign selling (30–60% of turnover): Forced exits by foreign funds — short‑term price dips are our buying opportunities. Low foreign selling / rising foreign buying: Selling is exhausting, local bid is absorbing it — this is the setup for a rally.

2.4 Bond Market Turnover

Bond turnover surging to TZS 20–40 bn/day = pension funds parking cash in fixed income. Collapsing below TZS 5 bn = that money is rotating into high‑dividend stocks (Strategy 4 signal).

3. THE FIVE CORE STRATEGIES

Strategy 1 – Shadow Market Making (Spread Capture)

What It Is

You buy shares below the current market price and sell them above your purchase price — capturing the bid‑ask spread. Because 98% of DSE investors cross the spread at market prices, and there are no professional market makers, anyone who places patient limit orders on both sides earns a predictable return for providing liquidity.

How to Do It — Step by Step

  1. Choose liquid counters. Minimum 5,000 shares/day with at least 50 deals. Primary engines: CRDB, VODA, DCB, NMB.
  2. Place a GTC limit buy order below the current best bid. Example: CRDB VWAP is 2,650; bid is 2,640. You place a buy at 2,630 and wait for a seller who needs immediate exit.
  3. Once filled, place a GTC limit sell order at a price covering round‑trip fees (2.0–2.4%) plus profit. For CRDB, sell at 2,760 — a 130‑shilling spread. Gross profit on 200 shares = TZS 26,000; net ≈ TZS 18,000–20,000.
  4. Never hold more than 5% of a counter's 20‑day average daily volume overnight. If CRDB trades 800,000 shares/day on average, your 200‑share position is trivially exitable.
  5. The T+2 rule. Shares settle 2 business days after purchase. You cannot sell on the same day. The cycle: buy → wait for settlement → sell → immediately reload with a new buy order (2–5 day cycle).

5. RISK MANAGEMENT – NON‑NEGOTIABLE RULES

  1. Always use limit orders. Never place a market order. A market order in a thin frontier market can cost you 2–5% in slippage instantly.
  2. Never hold more than 60% of the portfolio in one counter. Even CRDB.
  3. Keep at least 20–30% of the portfolio in cash or government bonds. This is your buffer. It lets you buy when others are forced to sell.
  4. Never touch a stock that trades fewer than 5,000 shares a day. You can check in, but you can't check out.
  5. Calculate all returns in US dollars to account for the depreciation of the Tanzanian Shilling. Your real wealth must grow in hard currency terms.
  6. Reinvest every shilling of profit. Do not withdraw until you have reached your target. This is how compounding works.

BoundaryThe exemption is lost if the company delists. Always verify that a stock remains listed before relying on this treatment.

Legal BasisIncome Tax Act, Chapter 332, as amended
The RuleDividends from DSE‑listed companies: final WHT of 5%, irrespective of the shareholder's residency. Unlisted dividends: 10% (resident) or 10–15% (non‑resident).
How to ExploitAccumulate high‑dividend counters and hold through the ex‑dividend date. A TCC dividend of TZS 1,050 nets TZS 997.50 listed vs. TZS 945 unlisted. Over decades, that 5‑point difference is enormous.
BoundaryYou must be on the share register on the record date. Verify with your broker that your CDS account reflects the holding.
3  |  WHT Exemption on Government Bond Interest (≥3 Years) — Finance Act 2021
Legal BasisFinance Act, 2021 (amending the Income Tax Act)
The RuleInterest on DSE‑listed government bonds with maturity ≥3 years is exempt from WHT. Short‑term T‑bills (35‑, 91‑, 182‑, 364‑day) remain subject to 10% WHT.
How to ExploitWhen the Bank of Tanzania auctions a 15‑ or 20‑year bond at 11–16% coupon, you receive every shilling tax‑free, semi‑annually. Secondary‑market capital gains on these bonds are also 0% CGT.
BoundaryExemption applies only to bonds listed on the DSE. Verify maturity and listing status before purchase.
4  |  WHT Exemption on Listed Corporate Bonds & Sukuk — Finance Act 2022
Legal BasisFinance Act, 2022 (amending the Income Tax Act)
The RuleInterest or profit distribution from DSE‑listed corporate bonds, municipal bonds, and Sukuk is exempt from WHT.
How to ExploitNMB Jamii Bond, CRDB Kijani Bond, KCB Mapato Sukuk, EFTA Bond — all pay gross. A 14% EFTA bond delivers 14% after tax vs. 12.6% if unlisted.
BoundaryExemption applies automatically once the bond is admitted to the DSE. Confirm listing status with your broker.
5  |  Corporate Action Price‑Band Expansion — DSE Trading Rules, June 2025
Legal BasisDSE Trading Rules, June 2025 amendments, approved by CMSA
The RuleDaily price‑variation cap expanded by an additional 5% for five working days following a corporate action (dividend announcement, rights issue, merger). A large‑cap (normally 5%) can move 10%; a small‑cap (normally 15%) can move 20%.
How to ExploitPre‑position 2–3 weeks before the AGM. On announcement day, place a limit sell order at or near the expanded band limit. After the five‑day window, the price frequently retraces — re‑enter at a lower price.
BoundaryExpanded band applies only to formally announced corporate actions. Trading on rumour alone is risky and unprotected.
6  |  VWAP Closing‑Price Mechanics — DSE Trading Rules, June 2025
Legal BasisDSE Trading Rules, June 2025
The RuleOfficial closing price = VWAP, provided ≥100 shares are traded in the session.
How to ExploitIn the final 30 minutes, execute a genuine sell order slightly above the developing VWAP. Your trade becomes part of the weighted average. Next morning, orders often cluster around the previous close, providing a slightly better opening.
BoundaryOrders must be real, capital‑backed trades. Never place an order solely to manipulate the closing price.
7  |  Rights‑Issue Discount Arbitrage — Companies Act / CMSA
Legal BasisCompanies Act and CMSA regulations on rights issues
The RuleExisting shareholders can subscribe to new shares at a price often significantly below market value.
How to ExploitMaintain TZS 200,000–500,000 cash in CDS account. Buy minimum qualifying shares before the record date. Subscribe to the new shares at the discount, then sell once credited (or hold). Example: MCB May 2026 at TZS 110 vs. market TZS 1,200+.
BoundaryYou must be a shareholder on the record date. Trading after the last day to trade cum‑rights entitles you to nothing.
8  |  Block Trade Information Advantage — DSE Block Trade Rules
Legal BasisDSE Trading Rules (block trade: single buyer, single seller, minimum TZS 250 million)
The RuleBlock trades are negotiated off‑market but reported to the market the following day.
How to ExploitThrough active broker relationships, you may learn of a pending block trade before it prints — market‑flow intelligence, not inside information. Position accordingly (accumulate if a premium block is expected; offer to be the counterparty).
BoundaryNever act on information about an impending corporate event before it is public. Block‑trade flow is transaction information, not corporate inside information.
9  |  NIDA‑Verification Friction — CMSA Directive, June 2025
The PhenomenonNIDA mandatory for CDS accounts (June 2025). During high‑backlog periods, new account openings slow — reducing retail participation and thinning the order book.
How to ExploitMonitor CMSA statistical bulletins. When new‑account growth stalls, accumulate at potentially better prices. When backlogs clear and accounts surge, sell into the renewed retail demand.
BoundaryMacro‑observation strategy only. All data is publicly available from CMSA publications.
10  |  ETF Price‑Deviation Arbitrage — DSE Listing Rules
The RuleiTrust EAC Large‑Cap ETF and Vertex ETF publish daily NAVs. Market price must not deviate >5% from NAV.
How to ExploitCheck the fund manager's website for the latest NAV. If the price is >3% below NAV, buy and wait for convergence. If at a premium, avoid buying or sell existing units.
BoundaryDiscount can persist for extended periods. Do not over‑allocate to a single ETF. Patience is required.
11  |  Reduced Corporate Income Tax for New Listings — Income Tax Act
The RuleNewly listed companies with ≥25% public float: reduced CIT of 25% (vs. standard 30%) for three consecutive years.
How to ExploitIdentify companies approaching an IPO. The reduced tax burden boosts post‑tax earnings and dividends. Subscribe to fundamentally sound IPOs to benefit from this structural earnings uplift.
BoundaryThe company must maintain the public float; if it falls below 25%, the incentive is revoked.
12  |  General Tax Optimisation Principles

The overarching principle: structure all investments to benefit from the most favourable tax treatment available.

  • Hold all Tanzanian equities in your CDS account, not in an unlisted vehicle.
  • For fixed income, prefer bonds with maturity ≥3 years over Treasury bills.
  • Reinvest all after‑tax proceeds; tax‑free compounding is the engine of long‑term wealth.
